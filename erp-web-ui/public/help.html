Northwood ERP — Demo Guide

Step-by-step for driving each demo through the web UI. Every step names the persona to sign in as and the sidebar path to follow. Open this any time from the ? icon in the top bar.

Getting started

  • Sign in at the Keycloak login screen. Password is the same as the username (e.g. sarah / sarah).
  • The left sidebar groups screens by module (Master Data, Sales, Inventory, Manufacturing, Purchasing, Finance, Reporting, System). Click a module to expand it.
  • The Home page shows quick links tailored to your role.
  • Action buttons you don't have the role for are disabled with a "Requires role: …" tooltip — that's the security model, not a bug.

Personas & switching

Each demo persona maps to one role. To switch persona mid-demo: click the Sign out icon (top-right), then sign back in as the new persona on the Keycloak form. The UI re-reads your role automatically.

Pages to keep on screen

These reporting/detail views update live as a saga advances — open one on a side screen and refresh:

  • Sales → Sales Orders → (a row) — the Sales Order detail roll-up (reservation → shipment → invoice → payment). The headline view.
  • Reporting → PO Tracking — ordered / received / invoiced / paid money bars.
  • Manufacturing → Production Board — per-work-order progress.
  • Reporting → Material Shortage — open → purchase requested → purchase ordered → resolved.
  • Reporting → Financial Dashboard — revenue, COGS, gross %, open counts.
  • Finance → Journal Entries — balanced debit/credit pairs landing per action.

Demo 1 — Product master ↑ top

emma The product service owns SKUs, pricing, reorder policy, and make-vs-buy. All edits are inline actions on the product detail page.

  1. Go to Master Data → Products and open Wooden Dining Table (FG-TABLE-001).
  2. Change pricing: click Change pricing → set sales price + standard cost → Save.
  3. Reorder policy: click Reorder policy → set reorder point + quantity → Save.
  4. Make vs buy: click Make vs buy → choose Manufactured / Purchased / Both → Save. (Drives whether replenishment routes to manufacturing or purchasing.)
  5. Approved vendors: open the Vendors tab → Edit vendors → tick approved suppliers, star a preferred one → Save. (The preferred vendor drives auto-sourcing on POs.)
  6. Discontinue: click Discontinue → confirm. The SKU can no longer be ordered, requisitioned, or produced.
Watch: changes ripple to the other services' projections within a second — e.g. a make-vs-buy flip changes whether the SKU appears in the BOM picker (manufactured) or the requisition picker (purchased).

Demo 3 — Sales-order fulfilment (happy path) ↑ top

A stock-covered order ships straight from inventory — no manufacturing involved.

  1. sarah Sales → Sales Orders → New. Pick customer Sydney Home Living (CUST-001), add 1 × FG-TABLE-001, Create. You land on the Sales Order detail page; with stock on hand the saga reaches ready_to_ship in one tick.
  2. mike Inventory → Shipments → New. Pick the ready-to-ship order, confirm. Finance auto-creates the customer invoice; the order's roll-up shows it.
  3. olivia Finance → Payments → New → Customer tab. Pick the customer invoice, record full payment. The order reaches completed.
Watch: the Sales Order detail roll-up walks reservation → shipment → invoice → payment as you go.

3.2 — Prepayment / cash-with-order

Same flow, money first. sarah place an order and (in the New form) set Payment terms = prepayment. The order shows an "awaiting prepayment" lozenge; a shipment is blocked until paid.

  1. olivia Finance → Payments → New → Customer — pay the prepayment invoice in full. The lozenge clears and fulfilment proceeds.
  2. mike ship it when the order reaches ready_to_ship. Finance reclassifies the deposit to revenue at shipment.
Watch: Finance → Journal Entries shows the deposit→revenue reclassification at shipment.

3.3 — Planning time fence (deferred release)

A product's planning time fence holds a far-future order back: instead of reserving stock the moment it's placed, the fulfilment saga waits until requested-delivery − fence days, then reserves as usual. 0 (the default) = no fence = reserve immediately. Useful for made/bought-to-order goods you don't want to commit stock to weeks early.

  1. emma Master Data → Products → open a product (e.g. FG-TABLE-001) → Planning fence → set 7 days → Save. (The Replenishment card now shows Planning Fence: 7 days.)
  2. sarah Sales → Sales Orders → New — order that product with a Requested delivery date far in the future (e.g. a month out). Unlike a normal order it does not march to ready_to_ship: it parks with no stock reserved (Stock stays pending) until a week before the delivery date.
  3. For contrast, place a second order for the same product with a near requested-delivery (inside the 7 days) — it reserves immediately and proceeds the usual way.
Note: there's no dedicated "Scheduled" pill yet — a parked order just shows header Submitted with Stock pending. The saga sits at awaiting_release (see docs/demo-script.md §1.6 for the exact SQL) and wakes itself on the release date without re-checking the fence (decide-once). A parked order is still cancellable: sam can cancel it before release — nothing was reserved, so compensation completes cleanly.
Watch: the far-future order sits idle on Sales → Sales Orders while the near-term one walks straight to ready-to-ship.

Demo 4 — Failure & cancel paths ↑ top

4.1 — Cancel an order (role gate)

  1. sarah place an order for more than is on hand so it parks at stock_reservation_incomplete.
  2. Still as sarah, open the Sales Order detail and hover Cancel order — it's disabled with "Requires role: sales_manager".
  3. sam sign out, sign back in as sam. Now Cancel order is enabled — click it, give a reason, confirm.
Watch: the roll-up walks compensating → compensated; the reservation is released and the order shows cancelled.
Note: cancel is rejected after the order has shipped (that needs a credit-note/return flow, out of scope).

4.1.1 — Cancel a paid deposit order → automatic refund

sarah place a deposit order and olivia pay the deposit invoice. Then sam cancel it before shipment — finance posts the refund automatically.

Watch: a green "refunded" lozenge appears; Finance → Journal Entries shows the customer_refund posting (Customer Deposits nets to zero).

4.2 — Reservation comes back short

sarah order more than on-hand of a makeable product. Inventory raises a make-to-stock work order to top up; the order parks at stock_reservation_incomplete and retries once stock arrives (continue with Demo 5). If the product has no active BOM (or no vendor), the order is rejected instead.

Demo 5 — Make-to-stock work order ↑ top

A work order appears when inventory dispatches a manufacturing-routed replenishment (from a shortage). Drive it on the board.

  1. linda Manufacturing → Production Board. Open the released work order.
  2. For each operation in sequence (10, 20, 30 …) click Complete operation. Each completion advances the work-order saga.
  3. When the last operation completes, on-hand is bumped and any parked sales order re-reserves and reaches ready_to_ship.
Note: if a BOM line is a sub-assembly, a child work order is created — complete its operations first; the parent holds until children finish.
Watch: Manufacturing → Production Board shows each work order walk released → in_progress → completed.

Demo 6 — Purchase-to-pay ↑ top

  1. tom Purchasing → Purchase Requisitions → New. The requisition number is auto-filled (editable). Add a purchased product (e.g. RM-LEG-001) with a quantity, Create. It auto-converts to a draft purchase order.
  2. priya Purchasing → Purchase Orders → open the draft PO → Approve (optionally add a note). It flips to sent.
  3. mike (or tom) Inventory → Goods Receipts → New. Pick the PO and its line from the picker, set received quantity, post.
  4. olivia Finance → Supplier Invoices → New. Pick the PO + goods receipt, confirm the lines, record. Finance runs the 3-way match (quantity + price). On match it auto-approves.
  5. olivia Finance → Payments → New → Supplier. Pick the approved invoice, record payment. The PO flips to paid.
Variance branch: record an invoice with a unit price > 2% off the PO price and it parks at three_way_match_failed. Review it at Finance → Pending Review — daniel can manual-approve or reject.
Watch: Reporting → PO Tracking — ordered / received / invoiced / paid bars accumulate; the Match column flips to Matched once the invoice is approved.

Demo 7 — End-to-end (all three sagas) ↑ top

One big order that touches every service. Pre-state: little/no FG-TABLE on hand and short raw materials, so the order triggers manufacturing and purchasing.

  1. sarah place an order for 10 × FG-TABLE-001 for CUST-001. It parks at stock_reservation_incomplete.
  2. Inventory raises a make-to-stock work order; releasing it hits a raw-material shortage, which inventory routes to purchasing as a PR → PO.
  3. priya approve the PO (if not auto-approved); mike post the goods receipt for the raw materials (Demo 6 steps 2–3).
  4. linda complete the work order operations on the Production Board (Demo 5). Finished goods land; the parked sales order re-reserves to ready_to_ship.
  5. mike ship the 10 units; olivia record the supplier invoice + pay it, and receive the customer's payment (Demos 3 & 6 final steps).
Watch (side screens): Sales Order detail, PO Tracking, Production Board, Material Shortage, Available-to-Promise, and Financial Dashboard all update as the three sagas run concurrently.

Demo 8 — Security & audit ↑ top

  • Login: open the app cold (or sign out) → you're redirected to Keycloak → sign in → the top-right chip shows your name + role.
  • Role gate: as sarah the Cancel order action is disabled (tooltip). Switch to sam and it's enabled. As aaron (auditor) every mutating action is disabled, but reading works everywhere.
  • Audit timeline: on a Sales Order detail page open View audit, or go to System → Audit Log. Every event is stamped with its source service, type, and actor (e.g. the cancel row shows sam; saga-driven rows show system).
  • Reverse a journal: as daniel open Finance → Journal Entries, pick a posted entry, click Reverse. (Disabled for olivia.)

Demo 9 — Broker outage (auto-recovery) ↑ top

Shows that nothing is lost if the message broker goes down — the outbox holds events and replays them on recovery.

Note: stopping/starting the broker is a terminal action, not a UI one — run docker stop northwood-kafka then later docker start northwood-kafka. The web UI is where you observe it.
  1. Open a Sales Order detail page for an in-flight order.
  2. Stop the broker, then sarah place a normal order — it still succeeds (the command path doesn't touch the broker), but the roll-up stalls at an early state because its events can't reach the other services.
  3. Start the broker again. With no restart or manual action, the roll-up resumes and marches the order through to completed — just delayed by the outage.

Demo 10 — Make / buy-to-order (pegged supply) ↑ top

Some SKUs are order-pegged (to_order): an order raises dedicated supply earmarked to that line, instead of drawing from the shared pool.

10.1 — Buy-to-stock baseline (contrast)

sarah order 1 × FG-RUG-001 (Woven Floor Rug). It has stock on hand, reserves from the pool, and goes straight to ready_to_ship — the ordinary off-the-shelf path. Keep it on screen next to 10.2.

10.2 — Buy-to-order (pegged PO)

  1. sarah order 1 × FG-CARPET-001 (Custom-design Carpet) — 0 on hand, to_order. The order parks while a dedicated purchase order is raised for exactly the line quantity, sourced to Floor Coverings Direct (SUP-006).
  2. priya approve the PR/PO (Demo 6 step 2).
  3. wendy (or mike) post the goods receipt. The received unit is reserved to the SO line in the same step — it never enters available-to-promise.
  4. The order goes stock_reservation_incomplete → ready_to_ship directly (ships off the peg, no re-reservation). mike ship it.

10.3 — Make-to-order (configure a SKU live)

emma take a manufactured FG (e.g. FG-CHAIR-001): on its product detail page set Reorder policy to 0/0, then set the replenishment strategy to to_order. Then sarah order it beyond on-hand — the same pegged flow, but the dedicated supply is a work order (complete it on the Production Board).

10.4 — Cancel a pegged order

sam cancel the carpet order (any pre-shipment state). The peg is released — if the goods already arrived they return to the free pool.

Watch: Inventory → Stock Balances — for a pegged product, reserved rises with on-hand and available stays 0; on cancel, reserved drops and available rises.
